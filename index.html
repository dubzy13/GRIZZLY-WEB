Cultural Impact & Media Appearances

The LAR Grizzly pistols have earned their place in popular culture, appearing in Hollywood blockbusters and video games as the ultimate expression of handgun power.

Film Appearances

The Shadow (1994)

Highly customized LAR Grizzly Mark I pistols in .45 Win Mag served as The Shadow's signature weapons, wielded by Alec Baldwin. These custom pistols, nicknamed "Silver Heat," were specially built by L.A.R. Manufacturing with extended slides and frames to resemble oversized period M1911 pistols. Four pistols were constructed specifically for the film, making them some of the most recognizable movie guns of the 1990s.

Terminator Salvation (2009)

The LAR Grizzly Mark I in .45 Win Mag appears in the post-apocalyptic future, used by Barnes (Common) and Blair Williams (Moon Bloodgood) to confront Marcus Wright. The film showcases the pistol's raw power as Barnes examines a .45 Win Mag round before loading it, emphasizing the Grizzly's place as a weapon of serious stopping power in humanity's fight against the machines.

Sniper (1993)

"El Cirujano" (Ken Radley) carries a LAR Grizzly Mark I in a shoulder holster throughout the film. Master Gunnery Sergeant Thomas Beckett (Tom Berenger) uses it in the film's climactic moments, showcasing the pistol's reliability in jungle warfare conditions.

Video Game Appearances

Phantom Forces (Roblox)

The Grizzly Mark V features as one of the most powerful handguns in the game, chambered in .50 AE. Players recognize it for its devastating damage profile and distinctive bear emblem, living up to its "Bear gun go boom" reputation.

Fallout 4 (Community Mod)

The LAR Grizzly Win Mag appears as a highly-detailed weapon mod, featuring unique variants and quests. The mod's description perfectly captures the Grizzly's essence: "An oversized 1911 on .45 Winchester Magnum steroids. The gun you didn't know you needed."

The Grizzly's appearances in media cement its status as an icon of American firearms engineering - a pistol that pushed the boundaries of what was possible in semi-automatic handgun design.

The LAR Grizzly Big Boar, developed in the 1990s by LAR Manufacturing in West Jordan, Utah, became one of the most iconic .50 BMG rifles ever made. Its distinctive bullpup design and breech-loading single-shot configuration made it instantly recognizable and incredibly compact for a .50 caliber rifle.

Hollywood Legend

The Big Boar achieved legendary status through its memorable appearances in major motion pictures:

  • Tremors 2: Aftershocks (1996) - Wielded by Burt Gummer (Michael Gross), who used it to devastating effect against Shriekers. In one unforgettable scene, a single shot penetrated a Shrieker, a concrete wall, wooden beams, oil barrels, an outhouse, and their escape vehicle - continuing for approximately five miles.
  • Tremors 3: Back to Perfection (2001) - The Big Boar made return appearances throughout the film.
  • The Lost World: Jurassic Park (1997) - Featured as tranquilizer rifles used by the InGen hunters.

Performance & Design

At just 45.5 inches overall length and weighing 30.4 pounds (without optics and tripod), the Big Boar was one of the most compact .50 BMG rifles available. The bullpup configuration kept the rifle short while maintaining a full 36-inch barrel for maximum velocity and accuracy. Despite firing the powerful .50 BMG cartridge, the innovative muzzle brake design and heavy construction made recoil surprisingly manageable - often compared to a 12-gauge shotgun.

The rifle featured a breech-loading bolt that had to be completely removed to load each round, AR-15 pattern grip and trigger group, elevated Picatinny rail for optics mounting, and came standard with a modified Harris bipod. Available in multiple finishes from matte black to stainless steel, each Big Boar was hand-fitted and built to exacting standards in Utah.
